This overlaps with Blog 2, but that is okay. Blog 2 is about the backtest high. Blog 3 is about what happened when you tried to make the system more practical and profitable.

Main content

This blog should focus on operational reality.

After seeing strong backtest results, you continued improving the system and trying to make it work in practice.

This is where you learned one of the biggest lessons:

Building a great system and executing it profitably are different games.

You faced problems like:

- Free RPC vs paid RPC differences
- Reliability issues
- Latency and data availability
- RPC cost becoming part of the business reality
- Gains Network alone not being enough
- Need to expand to GMX and Avantis
- Attempting Jupiter Perps and realizing Solana was a different world
- Jupiter Perps not being fully public in the same way
- Solana RPC infrastructure being much more expensive
- Strategy edges appearing, disappearing, or needing frequent fixes

This blog should feel like a builder slowly discovering that the real world is messier than the architecture diagram.

You had many small successes.

You found edges.

You fixed issues.

You improved strategies.

You learned more about platforms.

But the results were not as big as the dream from the backtest.

This is where the emotional tone should be mixed:

Excited, but tired.
Learning, but not satisfied.
Progressing, but not yet winning.
Core lesson

The key lesson is:

Profitability is not only a software problem.

It depends on infrastructure, data quality, execution timing, market structure, platform differences, cost, and discipline.

Suggested ending

End with:

“I was still making progress, but the dream became less simple. The system was improving, but the market was teaching me that execution is where ideas become expensive.”
