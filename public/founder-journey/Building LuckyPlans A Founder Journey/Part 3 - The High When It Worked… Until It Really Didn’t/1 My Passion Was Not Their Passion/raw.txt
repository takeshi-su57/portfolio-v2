Blog 1 — My Passion Was Not Their Passion Time period March 2025 – April 2025 Main content This blog should focus on what happened immediately after the MVP. You finished the MVP, and naturally the next thought was: “Now I need people who will walk with me.” You tried to contact potential traders, investors, crypto enthusiasts, and maybe companies. You were not just looking for users. You were looking for people who could believe in the vision. But the result was disappointing. The system was working, but your proof was not strong enough yet. You had passion. You had a working MVP. You had a story. You had technical progress. But for other people, that was not enough. They did not share the same emotional journey. They did not see the months of learning, the POC excitement, the VPS running at night, or the logs moving. They only saw a risky trading product without enough evidence. That is where the key lesson appears: A working system is not the same as a convincing system. Core lesson You learned that before asking others to trust LuckyPlans, you needed stronger evidence. Especially: - Backtesting had to be completed. - You had to become the first serious user. - You had to prove that the system could generate value for yourself first. - Passion alone could not bring money or users.
