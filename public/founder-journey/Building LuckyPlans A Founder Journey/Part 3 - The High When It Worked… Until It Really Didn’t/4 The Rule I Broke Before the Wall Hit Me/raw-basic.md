Main content

This blog is the final section of Part 3.

By this point, you had learned that Gains Network alone was not enough. So you revamped the system into V2.

The goal was much bigger:

- Multi-platform support
- Multi-collateral support
- Multi-chain support
- More flexible architecture
- Support beyond one perp DEX
- Better foundation for LuckyPlans as infrastructure

This was a serious technical upgrade.

But during that period, you also broke some of your primary rules.

You do not need to reveal every rule immediately. Keep some mystery for Part 4.

But you can say:

- I trusted the system too much.
- I moved too aggressively.
- I let confidence override discipline.
- I treated a technical upgrade like a validation of the whole strategy.

Then the result was painful.

You “bled” quickly. LuckyPlans faced a huge wall.

This should be the cliffhanger ending of Part 3.

The reader should feel:

“Okay, the system was getting bigger, but something serious went wrong.”

Then Part 4 can answer: bankrupt, restart, or keep building?

Core lesson

The more powerful the system becomes, the more important the rules become.

A bigger system does not protect you from bad decisions. Sometimes it amplifies them.

Suggested ending

End with:

“V2 made LuckyPlans stronger technically. But I was not stronger enough as the operator. That difference hit me hard. By September 2025, LuckyPlans had reached the wall I could not ignore anymore.”
