Blog 3 — The Detour That Taught Me How to Restart
Time period

March 2026 – May 2026

Main content

This blog should explain the unexpected outside experience that helped you return to LuckyPlans with a new point of view.

After the Optuna and validation work, you realized that finding a truly robust strategy would require much stronger research infrastructure.

You started planning:

Distributed Optuna optimization
Central orchestration
Parallel strategy search
Forward validation windows
More serious compute-heavy research

But before fully building that system, you had a chance to work with an Israeli founder building a cybersecurity product.

The project was an IGA platform connector layer, but not a normal connector system. The goal was to build an AI-agent-based connector platform that could learn IGA systems like SailPoint, study legacy application architecture, understand authorization logic, and generate highly accurate connectors from documentation and provided data.

This became important because it changed how you thought about building.

The biggest challenge was not simply using LLMs. The challenge was making LLM outputs accurate, repeatable, and reliable enough for enterprise workflows.

Through that work, you learned the modern AI-native development workflow:

Vibe coding
AI-assisted architecture
LLM agents
Claude/Codex-style development
Faster prototyping
Accuracy evaluation
Founder-level speed

This was very different from 2024.

When you started LuckyPlans, building the POC, MVP, V1, and V2 took almost a year.

By 2026, with modern AI tools, you realized a skilled solo founder could build a serious MVP much faster.

Not production-perfect overnight.
Not magic.
But fast enough to validate, demo, iterate, and rebuild with much less friction.

This gave you energy again.

LuckyPlans did not have to restart in the same slow, exhausting way.

You could restart with a new mindset:

AI-native development.
Faster iteration.
Better validation.
More disciplined strategy research.
Less blind confidence.
More evidence.

Core lesson

The first LuckyPlans journey taught you what not to trust too quickly.

The AI-agent work taught you how much faster and smarter the restart could be.

Suggested ending

“I did not come back to LuckyPlans because I forgot the pain. I came back because I finally had a better way to build after the pain.”
